Projects

High Energy Physics

In summer 2025 I completed a research project based on analysing of Gaussianity in NNPDF4.0 replicas and non-parametric reconstruction of Parton Distribution Functions using Kernel Density Estimation. My work was supervised by Professor Luigi Del Debbio

The Physics of Morphologically Diverse Bacteria in Moving Fluids

From September to November 2024 (10 weeks), I completed my senior honours project investigating how the curvature of a single bacterium affects its rotational dynamics in shear flow. My project was supervised by Dr. Tyler N. Shendruk and Dr. Gavin Melaugh.

Temperature Trend Analysis

From January 2023 to June 2023 I completed research with Professor Gang Chen at UCLA. The broad focus was looking at temperature data from 1980 to 2020 using statistical analysis and machine learning methods.

BB84 Protocol and Quantum Key Distribution

As part of the 115C quantum mechanics course at UCLA, I conducted research and wrote a paper explaining the BB84 protocol and quantum key distribution. I focused on how the protocol works and what implications it could have in the future, particularly with the advent of quantum computing.

Spam Email Classification

I completed this project for the Machine Learning For the Physical Sciences course at the UCLA in fall 2023. The aim of this project is to find the best machine learning model to classify emails as spam.

Grover's Algorithm

During a summer internship, I completed a self led project based on Grover's Algorithm. Using Python, I implemented a classical and quantum approach to the unstructured sort problem and testing the runtime. The results were in line with the literature; namely using Grover's Algorithm provided quadratic speed up with comparision with a classical method.

Using Standard Candles Data to Analyse the Lambda-CDM Model

Myself and a group of friends used type 1a Supernovae data to analyse the rate of expansion of the universe and conclude that the universe is expanding

Solar System Simulation

I created this project as part of the computer simulation course at the University of Edinburgh in my second year (2023). The project aims were to visualise the plantary orbits of the solar system and conduct different experiments related to energy conservation and integration techniques.
