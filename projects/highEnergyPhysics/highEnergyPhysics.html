Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs) play a crucial role in predicting outcomes of particle collider experiments, but their complex nature means they cannot be calculated directly and must be inferred from experimental data.

In this project, I focused on two main objectives. First, I evaluated how closely the NNPDF4.0 replica ensembles follow a Gaussian distribution by calculating the fourth standardised statistical moment (excess kurtosis), finding that most marginals approximate Gaussian behaviour. Second, I applied Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) to reconstruct PDFs non-parametrically, without assuming a specific functional form. The KDE-based reconstructions showed strong agreement with empirical data in one and two dimensions. By combining two dimensional covariance matrices I was able to construct a global correlation matrix (450x450) consistent with empirical data. This work contributes to improving our understanding of how making Gaussian assumptions in NNPDF4.0 data influence predictions of physical observables in particle physics.
