From January to July 2024, I conducted research under the supervision of Professor Gang Chen at UCLA, looking at Northern Hemisphere wintertime (December, January and February) surface air temperature variability from 1980 to 2020 using ECMWF Reanalysis v5 (ERA5) data.

During the winter quarter, I focused on learning foundational concepts in atmospheric physics and carried out baseline statistical analyses of the temperature datasets. This included evaluating the mean, variance, and skewness of surface air temperature fields, which provided a broad understanding of the data and a solid framework for subsequent work.

In the spring quarter, I extended this analysis by applying Empirical Orthogonal Function (EOF) analysis to identify the dominant spatial modes of temperature variability over North America and Eurasia. Then I used temporal autocorrelation and cross-correlation analyses of the Principal Components to investigate persistence and lagged relationships between these modes. This approach provided insight into the dynamics of sub-seasonal variability and demonstrated that the leading modes account for a significant fraction of the observed variability, with implications for improving sub-seasonal forecasts.
